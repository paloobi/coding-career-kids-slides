Software Engineer

Build things made out of code

Software = code that runs on a computer that people can use to do things.

Engineer = someone who designs and builds things. Lots of kinds of engineers (robotics engineers, design bridges). I’m the kind of engineer who designs and builds Software.

Front End? Engineer

Build the part of websites that live on your computer.

Well, we already know what an Engineer is.

The thing is, a website is built from a lot of different parts. Some of those parts are on your computer or phone - but some of them are on another computer somewhere else!

Every time you use the Web, your computer or phone is talking to a ton of other computers all at once just make the website work. Those other computers are usually called the “Back End” - so the “Front End” is the part of the website that runs on your computer or phone.

What part lives on your computer?

Well, a lot of stuff! Everything you see or hear on the website itself - text, images, links, videos. My job is to think about how things look, make sure the website is fast when you move around and try to do things, and hopefully making it fun and easy to use too!

Web Developer

Make websites! apps! games! and more!

Developer = This is basically just another word for Engineer, or for someone who makes things.

Web = Remember when I mentioned that your computer talks to a lot of computers all at once when you look at a website? Well the “Web” is the name we give for all of those computers talking to each other.

And the Web is not just websites! You can access the Web with apps on your smartphone or your tablet, when you talk to Alexa, when you play games, or use a virtual reality headset, or maybe even TV or your refrigerator!! All of those things are built by engineers like me.

What is code?

We know computers are powerful - there’s all sorts of things you can do with computers.
What are the ways you can tell your computer or your phone what to do? You can use a mouse You could type something in your keyboard You can use the touch screen on your phone You can talk to Siri or Alexa Your computer can do a lot of things already. But what if you want the computer to do something it doesn’t know how to do yet?

Writing Code = Teach Your Computer How To Do New Things

Your computer can learn to do things that it doesn’t know how to do yet, just like you. What makes coding a little tricky is that when you're talking to a computer, it doesn't understand you the same way a person would.
How do you make a peanut butter jelly sandwich?

  3. Spread jam on the other slice of bread 🍇

What happened here?? Unfortunately, even though computers can do a LOT, they really aren't as smart as you and me. The computer doesn't speak any human languages - it does not know what you meant when you said to put two slices together. And it can only do things it understands.

So if you want to teach the computer how to do things it doesn’t know how to do yet, you need to learn how to speak it’s language. And you need to figure out what to do when it doesn't understand what you tell it to do. That's what coding is all about

Sharing instructions in a language your computers understand. You can use code to teach your computer how to do new things!

Things Code Can Do

Video Games Medicine Government Theatre Writing & Books Movies Music Video Military Airplanes Spaceships Cars Robots!

So so so much! Code is everywhere - and Code is used in every single industry and field of study! Don’t believe me? Video Games - video games are made out of code Medicine - they use code for research and analysis to understand and discover new medicines Government - help people get services like healthcare or food stamps; and also to help the government understand the problems they’re trying to solve Theatre - code is used to run the lights and the sound for theatre productions! Writing & literature - code is used to format ebooks, to distribute books, and for bookstores and libraries to manage their collections Film & Movies - Visual effects, sound effects are made of code. Code also powers all the software used by screenwriters, the cameras used to film, and for things like video editing - at Netflix, code is also used to help people find movies and TV shows they want to watch Music - electronic music! You can even make music directly in your web browser with code! But code is also baked into almost all the equipment you see on stage at a concert - and many of the instruments, like electronic keyboards! Video - YouTube, Netflix, TikTok - all of these sites are made out of code! Streaming technology is really difficult, and there are still a lot of problems that need to be solved to make sure that the videos are able to load while you watch them. Military Defense - hackers from other countries sometimes try to attack our government or people’s computers! There are people whose job it is to defend us from those attacks using code! Also, the military has been at the forefront of many technological advances. Admiral Grace Hopper invented the first human-readable programming language - before her, you had to talk to computers in their language directly. Airplanes & spaceships - all of the equipment that makes airplanes and spaceships fly or navigate uses code. In fact, a woman named Margaret Hamilton led the team of Software Engineers who wrote the code that sent the first astronauts to the moon!! Cars - cars have computers inside them - and code is the thing that makes them keep working, and also lets the driver know if they need to be taken into the shop. Add now, with recent invention of self-driving cars, there is even more code being used to power cars! Factories - a lot of manufacturing is done with robots, and robots need to be coded so they can run properly. Even the tools used by other kinds of engineers - like architects - are made out of code.
